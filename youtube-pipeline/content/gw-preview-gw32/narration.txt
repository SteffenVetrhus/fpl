Number one. Erling Haaland. 22 goals on 21.06 xG -- he is the most reliable finisher in the league and he has Chelsea at Stamford Bridge. At 55% ownership, captaining him is safe but still the statistically correct call.

Number two. Bruno Fernandes at home to Leeds. United have a cushy FDR 2 fixture, and Bruno's form is absurd -- 9.2 form rating, the highest in the game right now. 189 total points. 10.32 xG plus 9.03 xA gives him the best combined attacking output of any midfielder. At 10.2 million and 44% ownership, he is a genuine armband contender this week.

If you are feeling brave, he might be the play over Haaland.

First differential: Harry Wilson. Fulham's midfielder has 10 goals on just 5.12 xG -- a clinical overperformance of plus 4.88. That is the second-best Goals minus xG in the league behind Semenyo. Wilson is in red-hot form at 6.4, recently rose 0.1 to 6.1 million, and Fulham travel to Liverpool. That sounds tough, but Wilson has been scoring in difficult fixtures all season. At just 24.9% ownership, he is a genuine differential captain shout.

Second differential: Welbeck. Brighton's striker has 12 goals on 10.28 xG and a form of 6.6. He is 6.1 million and only 4.5% owned. Brighton visit Burnley, FDR 2 for the away side. The price is absurd for that output. If you need a budget forward producing premium numbers, Welbeck is the answer.

Jean-Philippe Mateta. He tops the Big Chance Wasters list with an xG of 11.99 but only 8 goals. That is a minus 3.99 underperformance -- the worst in the league. Crystal Palace host Newcastle, FDR 3, which is not terrible on paper. But when a striker is missing nearly four goals' worth of chances, the underlying data is screaming regression. At 7.5 million and just 6.5% ownership, most of you have already moved on. If you have not, this is your sign.
